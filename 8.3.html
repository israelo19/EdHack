So what is the Pythagorean Theorem?

The Pythagorean Theorem helps us to figure out the length of the sides of a right triangle. If a triangle has a right angle (also called a 90 degree angle) then the following formula holds true: a2 + b2 = c2 Where a, b, and c are the lengths of the sides of the triangle (see the picture) and c is the side opposite the right angle. In this example, c is also called the hypotenuse.
